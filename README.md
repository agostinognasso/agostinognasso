## About me

I work at the intersection of **Statistics**, **Economics**, and **Machine Learning**, with a focus on making complex models understandable and actionable. My research sits primarily in **Explainable AI (XAI)** and **Explainable Machine Learning (XML)**, developing methods and open-source tools that bring transparency to algorithms, especially in economic and policy contexts.

I am a core member of the **[Bibliometrix](https://www.bibliometrix.org)** research team and the academic spin-off **[K-Synth](https://www.k-synth.unina.it)**, where we build software and statistical infrastructure for science mapping and knowledge discovery.

---

## Research interests

- Explainable Artificial Intelligence & Explainable Machine Learning
- Interpretable decision trees and tree-based models
- Statistical methods for economics and social science
- Bibliometrics, science mapping, and knowledge graphs
- R package development
